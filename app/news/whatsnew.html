Online for real - Application research fellowship



In March 2016 I've applied as a research fellow at Het Nieuwe Instituut (Rotterdam). Taking as a departure point the centennial anniversary of the Russian Revolution, Het Nieuwe Instituut’s 2017 program will explore revolution, reflecting on radical movements of the 20th century while also investigating the unstable landscape of the present and the unpredictable territories of the future.
Within its annual theme I wish to further my research, deploying different media to develop it's visual identity, physical or digital form and messages. And to explore visual languages that can effectively communicate the urgent as well as trivial, the mainstream as well as the niche and the prosaic as well as the poetic parts of all the projects subject matter. Fingers crossed!

Dear friends, farewell



After an intimate closing party with friends and family in Amsterdam last Friday, it’s time to salute you and everyone else that has been following us online. A sign of gratitude. One last present. Our musical testament.

As a final endeavour, we asked all SEKOIA team members to select 5 tracks that have stuck with them through the years. Tunes that fully embody the SEKOIA sound, songs with special stories and music that would directly take them back to special moments experienced in the last four years.

During a rainy January afternoon, Will Oirson, Florinsz Janvier and Vela Uniform got together in the studio and mixed all these tracks into one, massive 5 hour recording. Our final podcast and our last gift to you. Lots of recognisable tracks, a collection of classics. Songs that will stick with us for the rest of our lives. A podcast with different chapters. There’s something in here for everyone. Please enjoy it as much as we did creating it!

- SEKOIA Family

Online for real Montréal - first footage



Fascinated by online visual culture and user generated content, Jeisson Drenth has been studying how the democratisation of production influences the way internet users create and spread media content online.
This ongoing study focuses firstly on how the internet has changed from a static, top-down medium to a global phenomenon that enables people to get in touch with each other through network connection on multiple social platforms for countless reasons. Secondly it examines the impact that the new endless stream of online (moving) images has on visual culture in general.

To step away from the traditional format of the article, the study has set out to draw inspiration from a new type of research and different media. In a series of short interviews 'Online for real' explores how young adults that went online for the first time during their teens engage with others on the world wide web. What social platforms do they use and how do they present themselves online? When they 'share' do they produce, select, maybe even curate? Do they use network connection to engage intimate relationships and if so, what are they willing to share and what not?

Online for real, coming fall 2016
